Carnatic Poetry

The beginning of kannada writing is in the Rashtrakuta courts , Kavirajamarga was written at this time and its about the kannada speaking people and their culture. The literature continued to flourish under the chalukyas and hoysalas. It grew further in the Vijaynagara Empire where Krishnadevaray was a huge kannada literature patron.In the 20th century, the Navya (New) movement emerged, characterized by experimentation with form and content. Poets like Kuvempu, Bendre, and D. R. Bendre introduced modernist sensibilities into Kannada poetry.Some of the recent times include Girish Kanad and U.R Ananthamurthy

Yakshagana

Yakshagana is a traditional theatre, developed in Dakshina Kannada, Udupi, Uttara Kannada, Shimoga and western parts of Chikmagalur districts. Yakshagana is traditionally presented from dusk to dawn. Its stories are drawn from Ramayana, Mahabharata, Bhagavata and other epics from both Hindu and Jain and other ancient Indic traditions

Mysore Silk Saress

It is a mulberry silk based sarees.It grew in populance during the Vijaynagara Empire.After there fall it fell into a small decline before it re-growth during the mysore kingdom .It is mainly produced by the KSIC(Karnataka Silk Industries Corporation Limited) whose first factory was founded in 1912 by Sri Nalvadi Krishnaraja Wodeyar, the Maharaja of Mysore.

History

The first Mauryan Emperor Chandragupta Maurya died in Shravanbelgola in Hassan District around 298 BCE large imperial empires, the Badami Chalukyas, Rashtrakuta Dynasty and Western Chalukya Empire, who had their regal capitals in modern Karnataka region and patronized Kannada language and literature e Hoysala Empire at the turn of the first millennium. Art and architecture flourished in the region during this time resulting in distinctive Kannada literary metres and the construction of temples and sculptures adhering to the Vesara style of architecture

Mysore Kingdom

The Wodeyars of Mysore, former vassals of the Vijayanagara Empire, After the fall of vijayanagara empire Mysore became independent kingdom 17th-18th century. With the death of Krishnaraja Wodeyar II, Haider Ali, the Commander-in-Chief of the Mysore Army, assumed control over the region, until the rule of the kingdom was passed to Tipu Sultan,He ruled until he lost to the british.

Hoysala Architecture

It is the building style developed by the hoysalas during the 11th and 14th century.Large temples were made such as Chennakesava Temple at Belur, the Hoysaleswara Temple at Halebidu, and the Kesava Temple at Somanathapura.The hoysalas usually dedicated there temples to Shiva and Vishnu but some temples of jain heritage were also developed

Karnataka: A Tapestry of Splendor


Nestled amidst the verdant Western Ghats, Karnataka's beauty is a mesmerizing blend of lush landscapes, rich heritage, and cultural vibrancy. From the majestic ruins of Hampi to the tranquil backwaters of Coorg, each corner of the state offers a unique charm. Its diverse terrain encompasses mist-covered hills, cascading waterfalls, and expansive coffee plantations, inviting adventurers and nature enthusiasts alike. Karnataka's architectural marvels, such as the intricate Hoysala temples and the imposing Gol Gumbaz, stand as testaments to its glorious past. With its vibrant festivals, delectable cuisine, and warm hospitality, Karnataka captivates the heart and soul of every traveler, leaving an indelible imprint of its beauty.
