Hampi : Mystical charm in the heart of Karnataka.


Hampi also reffered to as Hampe(Kannada) is ancient city in modern day Karnataka situated at the border of the Tungabhadra river.It is mentioned in the Ramayana and the Puranas as Pampa Devi Tirtha Kshetra.It is adorned with awe inspiring ruins,majestic temples and a stunning landscape.It offers a glimpse into India's glorius past.


History


Hampi dates back to centuries,its mentions in the Ramayana made it a prominent pilgrimage site.During the 3rd century BCE it is said to have been under the Mauryas.In the 2nd Century CE it fell under the chalukya's who made several inscriptions of it.By the 10th century it became a center of religious and educational activities many grants where made at this time to the Virupaksha Temple.The Hoysala's started ruling Hampi in the 12th and 14th Century and many durga temples where made.Later it would become the capital of the Vijaynagara Empire where it would continue to prosper


Religious Significance


Hampi's religious significance stems from its association with the Vijayanagara Empire, epitomized by temples like the Virupaksha and Vittala. The Virupaksha Temple, devoted to Lord Shiva, is the oldest functioning shrine. The Vittala Temple, honoring Lord Vishnu, showcases intricate architecture, including a famed stone chariot. Other notable sites include the Hazara Rama and Achyutaraya Temples. Jain monuments like those on Hemakuta and Matanga Hills add diversity. Beyond temples, Hampi's landscape, including the Tungabhadra River and surrounding hills, holds spiritual resonance, inviting meditation and contemplation. This blend of heritage and natural beauty makes Hampi a revered destination for religious seekers and pilgrims.

Virupaksha Temple


The Kamakshi Amman Temple in Kanchipuram showcases exquisite South Indian architecture with its towering entrance gopuram, intricately carved vimana, and ornate sculptures depicting various deities and mythical beings. Surrounding the main sanctum are smaller shrines dedicated to other gods and goddesses, all adorned with elaborate carvings and frescoes. The temple's thousand-pillared hall, "Aayiram Kaal Mandapam," is a marvel of craftsmanship, featuring intricately carved pillars showcasing divine motifs. Overall, the temple's architecture is a captivating blend of artistic splendor and spiritual reverence.


Vittala Temple


The Vittala Temple stands as a masterpiece of architecture and devotion. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, this temple complex is renowned for its stunning craftsmanship and intricate carvings, epitomizing the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire. The centerpiece of the temple is the famed stone chariot, an iconic symbol of Indian artistry. Visitors are captivated by the musical pillars within the hall, which produce melodious tones when struck. Surrounding the main shrine are numerous mandapas (pillared halls) and subsidiary shrines, each adorned with elaborate sculptures and reliefs depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. Despite centuries of wear and destruction, the Vittala Temple continues to inspire awe and reverence, offering a glimpse into India's rich cultural heritage and architectural brilliance.
